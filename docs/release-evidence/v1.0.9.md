## Customer Policy Review

The default configured return category is `MerchantReturnNotPermitted`. Customer-facing Terms and checkout disclosure state that change-of-mind, preference, fit, and sizing returns/exchanges are not accepted after charge. This does not remove remedies for damaged, defective, incorrect, missing, delayed, canceled, or unfulfillable items; duplicate charges, processing errors, suspected fraud, event cancellation, or rights required by law remain separate.

The seven-day damaged-item contact request is a verification guideline, not a return window or waiver. The shipping section distinguishes estimates from guarantees and states that an unshipped physical order that misses its promised or applicable shipment period requires customer consent to delay or cancellation/refund, without an unapproved materially different substitute. The policy is linked before payment and from stable localized navigation anchors. Forks may intentionally configure a finite or unlimited return policy, but the customer copy, JSON-LD, and mirrored Worker fields remain driven by the same category; a no-returns configuration cannot publish a fictitious return window.

Review basis:

- [FTC online shopping guidance](https://consumer.ftc.gov/articles/online-shopping) says sellers should disclose return/refund policies and follow shipping representations.
- [FTC guidance for merchandise not received](https://consumer.ftc.gov/articles/what-do-if-youre-billed-things-you-never-got-or-you-get-unordered-products) describes revised shipping dates, cancellation choice, and refunds when merchandise cannot be shipped as represented.

This engineering review is not legal advice. The machine-translated Spanish Terms still require native/legal review before being treated as professionally approved legal copy.

## Audit Finding and Repair

The initial mobile Lighthouse audit found the home LCP above the executable 4,000 ms ceiling in two consecutive runs (`4,299.96 ms` and `4,222.92 ms`). The trace showed that non-production Jekyll builds rendered all 50 catalog records, including archived/private products, while production correctly rendered only public active/sold-out records. That environment drift inflated the local home document to about 330 KB and made the performance gate test a different public surface from production.

The repair introduces one shared storefront visibility predicate for both the home grid and the responsive LCP preload. Local and production builds now render the same 23 eligible cards, omit archived/private products, and emit an approximately 197 KB home document. The post-fix Lighthouse run measured the home at `2,799.77 ms` LCP with a `0.93` performance score; all configured category, Web Vital, total/image/stylesheet, and third-party resource ceilings passed for home, a representative product, and Terms.

No other automated accessibility, SEO, performance, security, i18n, or responsiveness defect remained after this repair. The macOS host and Whisper capability checks passed, but real VoiceOver transcript evidence was explicitly skipped because no recording was requested or supplied; automated evidence is not represented as a real assistive-technology session.
